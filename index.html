About Our Solar System

Our solar system consists of the Sun and all the objects that orbit around it. This includes eight planets, numerous moons, asteroids, comets, and other celestial bodies. The Sun's powerful gravitational field keeps all these objects in their orbits.

The Sun

Our star, containing 99.86% of the solar system's mass

Inner Planets

Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars - rocky worlds close to the Sun

Gas Giants

Jupiter and Saturn - massive planets made mostly of hydrogen and helium

Ice Giants

Uranus and Neptune - cold worlds with icy compositions

Amazing Facts

The Sun is so large that about 1.3 million Earths could fit inside it!

Jupiter has at least 79 moons, with new ones still being discovered!

One day on Venus is longer than its year! It takes Venus 243 Earth days to rotate once on its axis.
